The problem

Hospital data exists. Clinicians can't act on it fast enough.

Labs, vitals, diagnoses — it's all there. But it lives in silos, arrives in raw form, and leaves the interpretation entirely to doctors who are already stretched thin.

📋
Numbers without context
A single lab result means little on its own — what matters is the trend, the patient's normal baseline, and what the guidelines recommend. Right now, doctors piece this together by hand, every single time.
🔍
Guidelines no one has time to check
Medical guidelines are updated constantly. Under time pressure, doctors rely on memory. MIRA checks every finding against the current guidelines automatically, every time.
🏗️
Tools that demand a big switch
Most clinical AI tools ask you to move your data onto their platform first. MIRA connects to whatever system you already have — no switch, no downtime.
How it works

Five simple steps. One clear, trustworthy report.

Each step has exactly one job, and none of them guess — every step reads directly from your patient data or from published medical guidelines.

Step 01
Gathering the data
MIRA automatically looks through your hospital's records to find the patient information it needs. If your system is set up a little differently, it adjusts and tries again on its own — no one has to search for anything by hand.
Works with the system you already have
Step 02
Spotting patterns over time
MIRA looks at the patient's full history for each unusual result and checks the direction it's heading — a value that's been climbing for 72 hours is a far stronger warning sign than one single abnormal reading.
Catches worsening trends early
Step 03
Checking medical guidelines
MIRA searches a library of trusted, published medical guidelines — covering sepsis, kidney injury, diabetes, heart conditions and more — for the exact situation found in the patient's data. It understands context, not just keywords.
Always shows which guideline it used
Step 04
Writing up the findings
MIRA turns the data and guidelines into one clear report: Patient summary → Concerns found → What the guidelines say → Recommended next steps. Every sentence can be traced back to a real number or a real guideline.
Every claim traces back to real data
Step 05
The doctor has the final say
A final check looks for mistakes or mismatches with the guidelines — then the report pauses and waits for a doctor to approve it or ask for changes. Nothing is added to a patient's record without a doctor signing off.
Nothing reaches a patient without approval
