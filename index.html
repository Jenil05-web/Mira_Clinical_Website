The problem

Hospital data exists. Clinicians can't act on it fast enough.

Labs, vitals, diagnoses — it's all there. But it lives in silos, arrives in raw form, and leaves the interpretation entirely to doctors who are already stretched thin.

📋
Data without context
A creatinine of 2.6 mg/dL means little without knowing the trend, the patient's baseline, and what the guidelines say. Doctors have to piece this together manually, every time.
🔍
Guidelines no one has time to check
Clinical guidelines are updated constantly. Under time pressure, doctors rely on memory. MIRA cross-references every finding against current evidence — automatically.
🏗️
AI that demands a migration
Most clinical AI tools require you to move your data to their platform. MIRA connects to whatever database you already have — PostgreSQL, MySQL, FHIR, or Excel.
How it works

Five agents. One grounded clinical report.

Each agent has a single job. None of them guess — they read from your data and cite from published guidelines.

Agent 01
SQL Data Extractor
Writes and executes a SQL query against your hospital's database. Automatically retries if the schema doesn't match, using the error as feedback. No manual query writing required.
Works with any SQL database
Agent 02
Lab Trajectory Analyst
Pulls the patient's full history for each abnormal lab and detects directional trends — a creatinine climbing over 72 hours is a far stronger signal than a single abnormal reading.
Catches worsening trends early
Agent 03
Semantic Cross-Reference
Searches a vector index of clinical guidelines (Surviving Sepsis, KDIGO AKI, ADA, ACC/AHA) using the actual lab values found — not a generic keyword search.
Cites source and guideline version
Agent 04
Clinical Reasoning
Synthesises patient data and guidelines into a structured report: Patient Summary → Identified Concerns → Guideline Context → Recommended Actions. Every claim is traceable to a source.
No hallucinated values
Agent 05
Critic + Clinician Review
A safety agent checks for hallucinations and guideline mismatches — then the report pauses for a clinician to approve or request revision. Nothing reaches the patient record without a doctor signing off.
Human-in-the-loop by design
What you get

Built for the clinical environment, not against it.

Core differentiator
Connects to your existing database
MIRA auto-discovers your schema using concept mapping — it finds your patient table, your lab results, your reference ranges, regardless of what they're named. No migration. No data leaves your servers.
Standard compliance
FHIR R4 — Epic & Cerner ready
For hospitals with modern EHRs, MIRA connects via HL7 FHIR R4 — the standard all major EHR vendors are now legally required to expose in the US and increasingly in India.
Regulatory
HIPAA-compliant audit trail
Every query, every agent decision, every human review is logged in an append-only audit table. Who accessed what data, when, and what the outcome was — always traceable.
Access control
Role-based access for your team
Clinicians can query and review. Admins configure the database connection and see usage statistics. Departments stay isolated. One login per doctor, managed by your IT team.
